Epistemic Matrix & Objective Assumptions

Investigating intelligence requires clear boundaries between facts and deductions. We separate findings into three layers.
Stated (Facts on the page)
  • The document is teletype radar track message traffic, compiled into a single record. p. 1
  • Altitudes appear in both feet and meters. Speeds appear in knots and km/h. p. 5 p. 280
  • A foreign air defense network scrambled MiG fighter jets to intercept tracks. p. 9 p. 236
  • The classification banners read TOP SECRET UMBRA. Redactions cite EO 3.3 and Public Law 86-36. p. 1 p. 250
Inferred (Logical deductions)
  • The use of two separate unit systems implies the involvement of at least two distinct reporting sources. p. 278
  • The metric radar track logs cluster in a single block, indicating they belong to one source system. pp. 278-286
  • The analyst's balloon labels are judgments, not physical confirmation. They are inferred from altitude and speed behaviors where target data was missing. p. 278
  • The redactions protect collection methods, sensors, and locations. The phenomenon descriptions are left intact, pointing to sources-and-methods withholding. p. 9
Speculation (Unproven ideas)
  • The deployment of MiG interceptor aircraft and metric units points to Soviet-aligned or Warsaw Pact air defense networks. The country of origin remains unnamed. p. 9 p. 278
  • The military intent of the balloon launches remains unconfirmed on the page. They may represent electronic warfare, meteorological probes, or atmospheric drift targets. p. 66
